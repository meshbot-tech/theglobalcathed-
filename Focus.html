Project One

Church Planting

The Church uses analogy of animals to describe a Church planyting Movements because the people understand the name of animals in the areas where we serve.

  • 1. Rabbits

    • The rabbit is a small animal that requires very small food to survive, lives in very dry areas, and yet multiply faster by giving birth to about 12 babies 4 times a year.

    The Global Cathedral uses this analogy to describe small Churches that are planted in the Rural areas where there are no roads, and no much civilization But yet these small churches are planted everywhere in villages, under the trees while some of them have small 20×40 tin builds which are usually used as worship places on Sundays to run worship services and the same buildings are used as schools in midweek for all children in the community.

    The Global Cathedral uses this analogy to describe small Churches that are planted in Rural areas where there are no roads, and no much civilization

    But yet these small churches are planted everywhere


  • 2. Rhino

    • The Rhino is a big animal that requires a lot of grass daily, water, much security and yet it multiplies slowly by giving birth to one baby every four to five years.

    The Global Cathedral uses this analogy to describe its church planting mega churches in the capital cities of Africa.

    These mega churches requires a highly professional staff team, large places of worship and modern facilities that assist in reaching the generation of young people.

    Project Two

    Leadership Development

    The Global Cathedral believes one of the greatest need of the church globally today is leadership.

    So as our contributions to this needs The Global Cathedral trains thousands of leaders each year.

    Through our school of ministry, leadership forums and annually The Global Cathedral runs one of the largest leadership conference in Africa dubbed Africa revival Movement (FREM).

    Project Three

    Humanitarian Projects

    The Global Cathedral supports a community water project led by Bishop Turere and his wife, Lucy Turere, in the semi-arid regions of Kajiado along the border of Kenya and Tanzania. This life-changing water project provides clean water daily to nearly 200,000 people.

    To witness the impact of this life-giving mission and learn more about the water project, visit our initiatives page.
