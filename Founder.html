Our Founder

Founder & Senior Pastor Bishop Moses Turere The Global Cathedral, Nairobi
Founder's Story

Bishop Moses Turere founded this church in the year 2000 as kiserian Baptist Church through the partnership between Bishop Turere and Parklands Baptist Church and thereafter in 2005 the church changed its name to Global Worship centre.

19th years later in June of 2019, still through the partnership and collerboration between Global Worship Centre in Kiserian and Parklands Baptist Church, Global Worship Centre started its first branch (The Global Cathedral) in Nairobi with the purpose of reaching the unchristian as well as equipping the Christian with the faith that works in real life.

Through its annual National Dedication services The Global Cathedral draws large crowds of up 32,000 people in single service including the head of State who grace these services.

Through his international partnerships, Bishop Turere has opened up education opportunities overseas to many students who are currently furthering their education in other countries.

Bishop Moses Turere, A soul winner, a leadership development specialists and a church planter having planted over 300 churches across East Africa. Trained at Baptist Theological seminary Bishop Turere is one of the most celebrated preachers in kenya

Alongside the church' main mission work of winning souls, training leaders, and planting churches, Bishop Turere has changed the lives of so many people through his philanthropic humanitarian projects such as providing clean water in semi arid lands serving 200,000 people daily, contacting mobile medical clinics in communities where people have no access to health care, rolling out large relief programs that feeds over 100,000 people in need of food and helping the pastoralists communities in restocking their livestock after perennial draughts.

Through his international partnerships, Bishop Turere has opened up education opportunities overseas to many students who are currently furthering their education in other countries.

To Many people and believers who looks for hope, and honesty from a church leader, Bishop Turere provides that best example.

Born and raised from a humble family of traditional seers (Oloibon) in rural part of kajiado a young man responded to God's call and through his faithfulness in God's service, truthfulness and honesty in dealing with people, Bishop Turere has rose through the ranks to become a national asset, beacon of hope and unifying figure who genuinely loves people regardless of their ethnicity and social classes.

Bishop Turere caught the National attention when he started the annual Mega prayer service at KICC every last Sunday of January which has attracted large crowds up 32,000 people including the head of state who annually graced the Mega prayer service.

Bishop Turere is Married to Pastor Lucy Tinga and both of them are blessed with two daughters Joy Tinga and Desire Tinga.

Our team is proud to present a vissionary church leader with a high reputation and a heart for the less privileged.
