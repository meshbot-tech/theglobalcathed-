Board Governance

The Board of Directors meets quarterly to review the church's strategic direction, financial performance, programme effectiveness, and governance compliance. The Board is independent of the senior pastoral team and provides objective oversight and accountability.

  • Quarterly board meetings with formal minutes
  • Annual strategy review and planning sessions
  • Sub-committees for Finance, HR, and Programmes
  • Annual board evaluation and performance review

Pastoral Council

The Pastoral Council — comprising the Senior Pastor, Associate Pastors, and Ministry Directors — meets monthly to coordinate ministry operations, resolve pastoral matters, and align all ministry activities with the church's vision and values.

  • Monthly pastoral council meetings
  • Bi-weekly leadership team check-ins
  • Annual leadership retreat and vision casting
  • 360-degree leadership accountability processes

Constitutional Framework

The Global Cathedral operates under a formal church constitution that governs membership, leadership selection, financial management, conflict resolution, and church discipline. The constitution is reviewed every 3 years to ensure it remains current and effective.

Compliance & Legal

The Global Cathedral is a duly registered organisation in Kenya, fully compliant with all applicable legal requirements including NGO registration, tax compliance, employment law, and corporate governance standards.

Financial Management System

All church finances are managed through a dedicated accounting software system with dual-authorisation controls, real-time reporting, and full audit trails. No financial transaction can be executed without dual approval from authorised signatories.

  • Monthly management accounts prepared by in-house accountant
  • Quarterly financial reports presented to the Board
  • Annual financial statements prepared to IFRS standards
  • Annual external audit by an independent registered firm

Offering & Tithes Management

Every offering collected at Global Cathedral is counted by a minimum of three independent counters, recorded in the offering register, and deposited in the church's bank account within 24 hours. Electronic giving through M-Pesa and bank transfer is fully reconciled weekly.

Budget & Expenditure Controls

The church operates on an annual budget approved by the Board of Directors. All expenditures must be budgeted, authorised by the relevant department head, and approved by the Finance Manager. Capital expenditure above KES 50,000 requires Board approval.

Donor Accountability

Every designated offering (building fund, KICC, missions, humanitarian) is maintained in a separate ring-fenced account and can only be used for its designated purpose. Donors receive formal receipts and annual reports on how their gifts have been used.

Sunday Services Operations

Each Sunday service is managed by a dedicated service coordinator who oversees ushers, children's ministry, sound and technical teams, parking, security, and medical standby. A detailed service run sheet is prepared for every service and reviewed in a pre-service briefing.

Events Management

All church events, from weekly services to the annual KICC convention, are managed by a dedicated events team with a detailed project plan, budget, assigned responsibilities, and post-event review. KICC planning begins 12 months in advance.

Facilities Management

The church premises are maintained to the highest standards of safety, cleanliness, and functionality. A facilities manager oversees maintenance schedules, security protocols, equipment servicing, and capacity management.

Safety & Security

Comprehensive safety and security protocols are in place for all gatherings, including trained security personnel, medical first responders, child protection policies, and emergency response procedures.

Staff Recruitment & Onboarding

All staff and pastoral appointments follow a structured recruitment process including application, interview, reference checks, background verification, and a formal onboarding programme. Every staff member receives a formal employment contract, job description, and performance targets.

Performance Management

Staff performance is reviewed quarterly through a structured appraisal system. Annual performance reviews determine salary adjustments, promotion decisions, and training needs. Underperformance is managed through a documented performance improvement process.

Training & Development

Global Cathedral invests in the continuous development of all staff. An annual training calendar covers theological development, leadership skills, technical skills, and wellbeing. Staff are encouraged to pursue further education with church support.

Child Protection Policy

Global Cathedral has a comprehensive Child Protection Policy that governs all interactions between adults and children within church programmes. All children's ministry workers undergo background checks and formal child safeguarding training.

Church Management Software

We use a dedicated church management system for member records, attendance tracking, small group management, giving records, and pastoral care follow-up. All data is stored securely with role-based access controls.

Digital Communications

Our communications team manages the church website, social media platforms, email newsletter, WhatsApp broadcast lists, and SMS communications. A content calendar ensures consistent, high-quality digital communications throughout the year.

Live Streaming & Media

All Sunday services and major events are live-streamed on YouTube and Facebook with professional multi-camera production. Sermon archives are maintained on the church's digital platforms for members and visitors to access on demand.

Data Protection & Privacy

Global Cathedral complies with Kenya's Data Protection Act. Member data is collected with explicit consent, stored securely, used only for stated purposes, and never shared with third parties without consent.
