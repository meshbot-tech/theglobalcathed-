Sisters In Touch

Women's Ministry

Sisters in Touch is our women’s ministry, headed by the bishop’s wife, Pastor Lucy. Sisters in Touch is a tremendous women’s ministry that started some time back in the rural areas, and we carried the same concept to the Rhino Church. It began in the Rabbit Women church planting movement, and we later carried it into the Rhino church planting movement.

It is an incredible women’s ministry, and the women in our churches form a very strong part of the ministry. They help the church to grow, disciple the young girls, and have faithfully supported the church’s catering department for many years, ensuring that everybody has something to drink and something to eat after every worship service.

For many people who desire food, whether lunch or supper, the one place they are certain they can receive something to eat is during our worship services, courtesy of the Sisters in Touch women’s ministry.

From the young professional navigating a new city, to the mother shaping the next generation, to the grandmother who carries decades of wisdom — every woman has a place in Sisters In Touch.

The Men Conqueror

Men's Ministry

Men Conqueror is our men’s ministry. We have been greatly blessed that our ministry has more men than women. Therefore, we bring them together, minister to them, and mentor them under the men’s ministry known as Men Conqueror, or simply, the Conquerors.

This is a ministry that brings together men of all ages, where they can discuss their issues, worship together, and build one another up. The older men are able to mentor the younger men, making it a tremendous platform for fellowship.

Through men's fellowships, accountability groups, leadership retreats, sports outreach, and the unapologetic preaching of the Gospel, Men Conqueror Ministry is challenging men to step up, step out, and step into the fullness of their Kingdom calling.

We believe that when men are transformed, families are transformed. When families are transformed, communities are transformed. And when communities are transformed, nations are transformed.

The 12 Tribes

Community Cell Groups

The Tribes. The Tribes is a ministry within our church that we use as our form of home fellowships, our cell groups, and our small groups. The name “Tribes” is derived from the 12 sons of Jacob, starting from the oldest to the youngest.

We group the whole church into 12 small groups according to the month of birth. For example, all the people who are born in January belong to one group called the Tribe of Reuben, the first son of Jacob. Then it goes like that all the way to December.

That is the ministry of Tribes, and this ministry helps our church to organize itself. It helps us to manage the people, get to know their talents, and bring people together according to generational gaps, because we have older people in the same tribe and younger people in the same tribe.

Our Children’s Ministry is truly an incredible ministry. With its three age-based groups and carefully written, well-researched material, it has been refined over the years by professionals and experts in children’s ministry.

Sunday School

Children's Ministry

Our Children’s Ministry is an incredible ministry divided into three groups: ages 0–7, ages 8–12, and up to 20 years. We use a written manual every quarter of the year, producing a manual for each group to use in their classes and activities.

We also have very well-trained teachers who are able to meet the needs of both the rural setting and the urban setting, including the city of Nairobi, as well as the modern lives of children who are more integrated into the global community than ever before.

Our trained, passionate teachers and children's workers are committed to making faith come alive for every child — through creative teaching, worship, prayer, and community. We partner with parents to disciple children at home and in church.

From babies and toddlers to primary school children, every child is known by name, loved unconditionally, and pointed to Jesus.

Youth Ministry

The Global Cathedral Youth Community

Our ministry is made up largely of young people, with nearly 70% of our congregation consisting of youth, many of whom are still in university pursuing their first degree, while others are undertaking postgraduate studies. Because of this, we have a dedicated Youth Ministry.

The Youth Ministry has a very strong leadership team, supported by a council. This council consists of one of the elders from the church’s Board of Elders who is specifically assigned to the Youth Ministry to ensure that the young people are mentored, strengthened in the ways of the Lord, and kept strong in their walk with God as they seek to change the world for Jesus.

The Youth Ministry also hosts an annual conference called the Africa Youth Summit.

Praise & Worship Ministry

Worship Ministry

Praise and Worship is a music ministry that does an incredible job of leading our congregation in worship, preparing the congregation for the Word, and helping people connect with God. It consists of highly skilled, talented young people who serve faithfully through music and worship.

Our worship teams serve across all Sunday services, special events, KICC conventions, and outreach gatherings. Musicians, singers, sound engineers, and creatives work together to create an atmosphere of worship excellence that is both technically superb and spiritually authentic.

Our Praise and Worship ministry is one of the great strengths of our church and plays a vital role in helping people connect with God during the time of worship. It is also one of the four main pillars that govern our worship services: Welcome, Worship, Preaching, and Fellowship. Therefore, the Praise and Worship ministry helps us to fulfill the pillar of worship within every service.
